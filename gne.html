Adrianna's Story

Adrianna, age 5 lives with her parents in Denver. Her dad works a a call-center, six days a week. The family has a newborn on the way, and Adrianna's dad fears he will miss the birth of his second child.

Describing their situation, Adrianna's parents shed frustrated tears. Their budget is tight and they constantly have to choose between vitaly important expenses. They have each often skiipped meals in order to provide enough food for Adrianna.

Adrianna's Story

Adrianna started kidergarten this year, and it's an immense relief to the family budget, since she'll be eating breakfast and lunch at school. Knowing with certainty that school meals will provide two of Adriana's meals each day will help the family stretch their limited food dollars when she's not in school so she gets the nutrition she needs

"If we didn't have federal nutrition programs my kids would starve. We would starve"

-Brandy, Adrianna's mom

About hunger in america

From city neighborhoods to rural farmstead, families in every community acrss the nation struggle to put food on the table, or are limited to cheap, unhealthy foods.

The problem isn't lack of food

This country produces more than enough food to feed every citizen. In fact, as much as 40% of the food we produce goes to waste.

The problem isn't lack of money

We are on of the world's wealthiest nations, with ample resources to feed those without enough to eat.

The problem is lack of willingness to end hunger in America.
